DCVLR: Data Curation for Vision Language Reasoning

A NeurIPS 2025 Competition focused on advancing visual reasoning capabilities in vision-language models through high-quality data curation.

Competition Overview

Reframing data curation as the primary variable for scientific investigation in vision-language reasoning

Challenge

Participants will be provided with a pool of 1 million image-text pairs and tasked with generating a small (1K) or large (10K) instruction-tuning dataset using any method of their choice.

Evaluation

Submissions will be evaluated by fine-tuning a fixed VLM (Molmo) on the curated data and measuring performance on VMCBench, a benchmark of multiple-choice visual reasoning questions spanning six diverse datasets.

Impact

This challenge reframes data curation as a scientific discipline with implications for adapting foundation models to real-world domains such as education, biomedicine, and scientific reasoning.

Technical Specifications

Detailed requirements and specifications for the competition

ü§ñ Model Architecture

Base Model: Molmo-7B-D

  • 7 billion parameters
  • Vision encoder: ViT-L/14
  • Language model: Qwen2-7B
  • Multi-modal transformer architecture
  • Pre-trained on diverse vision-language tasks

üíª Hardware Requirements

Minimum Specifications

  • GPU: NVIDIA A100 40GB or equivalent
  • RAM: 64GB system memory
  • Storage: 500GB available space
  • CUDA 11.7+ and PyTorch 2.0+
  • Linux OS (Ubuntu 20.04+ recommended)

üìä Dataset Specifications

Data Pool Details

  • 1 million image-text pairs
  • Images: 224x224 to 1024x1024 resolution
  • Formats: JPEG, PNG, WebP
  • Text: English captions, 5-200 words
  • Total size: ~150GB compressed

‚öôÔ∏è Training Configuration

Default Settings

  • Fine-tuning epochs: 3
  • Batch size: 32 (adjustable)
  • Learning rate: 2e-5
  • LoRA rank: 16 (optional)
  • Mixed precision training supported

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the DCVLR competition

What is the goal of this competition?

The goal is to advance the visual reasoning capabilities of vision-language models through instruction-tuning dataset curation. By isolating data as the primary variable, we aim to understand what data properties catalyze reasoning capabilities in neural systems.

Who can participate?

The competition is open to everyone, from students and academic researchers to industry professionals. We especially encourage participation from diverse backgrounds and institutions.

What computational resources do I need?

Fine-tuning and evaluation can be done on a single GPU. We provide $400 USD in GPU credits from Lambda Labs for each participant to ensure accessibility.

How will submissions be evaluated?

Submissions will be evaluated by fine-tuning a fixed VLM (Molmo) on the curated data and measuring accuracy on VMCBench, a benchmark of multiple-choice visual reasoning questions.

Can I use external data or models?

You must use only the 1M provided image-text pairs as your base data. However, you may use external tools (e.g., LLMs) to synthesize instruction-tuning examples from these pairs.

Are there any prizes?

Yes! Total prize pool of $25,000 USD. First place: $10,000, Second place: $7,500, Third place: $5,000. Winners also receive podium presentations at NeurIPS, invitation to co-author a joint system paper, and additional GPU credits. Top 10 teams receive recognition certificates.

How many submissions can I make?

Each team may submit up to 3 final versions. The highest-scoring submission will be used for ranking.
