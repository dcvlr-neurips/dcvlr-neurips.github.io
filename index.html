Overview

Reasoning models have become one of the most exciting areas of AI research. But most existing models are trained on data that is not publicly available, making it difficult to reproduce results and build on them. Furthermore, the modality of most frontier reasoning models remains language-only. But with your help, that is about to change! DCVLR, to be hosted at NeurIPS 2025, is the first open-data, open-models, open-source competition for data curation in vision-language reasoning. DCVLR is the first data-centric competition to be --

üåüOPEN-DATAüåü: The winning datasets from the competition will be made publicly available.

üåüOPEN-MODELSüåü: Unlike previous competitions, we are also evaluating the utility of reasoning data for frontier open-source models such as AI2's Molmo.

üåüOPEN-SOURCEüåü: All of the code in the competition will be publicly released to enable future research.

üåü Open Science

DCVLR is an open science competition; open data, open models, open source. Winning methods, datasets, and teams will be published and promoted to advance the field. We also aim to make the competition more accessible to students and hobbyists; teams submit the dataset, we train and evaluate the model, saving them the cost of compute. Student-lead teams can also apply for free compute from Lambda Labs, reducing barriers to entry and making cutting-edge research accessible to everyone, everywhere.

üéØ Challenge

Participants can leverage any source datasets to curate high-quality instruction-tuning datasets (1K or 10K examples). Participants are encouraged to explore diverse curation strategies, from synthetic data generation to subset selection. Submissions will be evaluated by fine-tuning an undisclosed, open-weights or open-source vision-language model (QwenVL 2.5, Molmo-O, InternVLM3 or similar) on the curated data and measuring performance across a wide variety of reasoning benchmarks (MathVista, VMCBench, LiveXiv or similar).

üî¨ Research Impact

What properties of training data drive reasoning capabilities in vision-language models? By isolating data curation as the primary experimental variable, participants will generate novel insights into the relationship between data characteristics and model performance‚Äîcontributing to the emerging science of data-centric AI.

Eligibility & Requirements

Who can participate and what you need to know

üåç Who Can Participate

  • Up to 500 teams of size 1-20 can compete
  • Anyone can join a team; students, researchers, and industry professionals are particularly welcome
  • No model training experience required; just curate and submit a dataset!
  • Participants from all backgrounds encouraged; it's free to enter and free to compete.

üìã Competition Rules

  • BYOD (Bring your own data) - use any dataset for training data curation
  • Data curation code must be open-sourced after competition
  • Teams must submit a write-up documenting their reproducible, scalable curation strategy
  • Teams must open-source any code used for data curation and submit a link to the GitHub repo
  • Detailed rules will be provided during the team signup period

üö´ Restrictions

  • Each individual can join only one team
  • (Free) OpenReview account signup required
  • Each team can submit a maximum of three datasets (1k or 10k tracks) to the leaderboard
  • Teams must submit datasets for content moderation and filtering (hateful and toxic content, decontamination with test sets, etc.) to compete

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the DCVLR competition

What is the goal of this competition?

The goal is to advance the visual reasoning capabilities of vision-language models through instruction-tuning dataset curation. By isolating data as the primary variable, we aim to understand what data properties catalyze reasoning capabilities in neural systems.

Who can participate?

The competition is open to everyone, from students and academic researchers to industry professionals. We especially encourage participation from diverse backgrounds and institutions.

What computational resources do I need?

Fine-tuning and evaluation can be done on a single GPU. We provide GPU credits from Lambda Labs for a limited number of participants to ensure accessibility.

How will submissions be evaluated?

Submissions will be evaluated by fine-tuning an undisclosed vision-language model on the curated data and measuring performance across a wide variety of benchmarks. This approach tests whether good data curation strategies generalize across different models and evaluation settings.

Can I use external data or models?

You can use any datasets you want for data curation. We provide a comprehensive starter kit to help you get started, and you may use external tools (e.g., LLMs) to synthesize instruction-tuning examples. The key constraint is no manual annotation.

Are there any prizes?

Yes! Total prize pool of $6,000 USD. First place: $1,500, Second place: $500, Third place: $250. Winners also receive podium presentations at NeurIPS, invitation to co-author a joint system paper, and additional GPU credits. Top 10 teams receive recognition certificates.

How many submissions can I make?

Each team may submit up to 3 final versions. The highest-scoring submission will be used for ranking.
